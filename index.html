About Me

I started learning programming in Middle School. Although conceptually the subject seemed fascinating, Pascal and VB did not excite me to much. It just seemed boring! As a result, I barely did any coding. Not until many years later, when I began learning OOP, and notorious C++. Pointers arithmetic, and other low-level features of the language helped me to appreciate Java with its garbage collection. Java was cool for a while, but I still ceased to see who exactly I want to be as a programmer. Everything changed when getting tired of constant updates, I migrated from Windows to Linux. CLI and idea of open-source software turned my world upside down!


At this point, I already had some basic knowledge of HTML and CSS, but no conceptual knowledge of the Web whatsoever. Browsing through open-source projects sparked a desire to learn more. That desire remains to the present day...


Today, I am a web developer, specializing in back end development, and mostly work with well known JS runtime, Node. That said, scope of my interests is not limited within this particular field, and includes blockchain technology, and data mining.


Currently enrolled in a 4 year Computer Science undergraduate program at San Francisco State University. Interested in internship or research opportunity that would allow me to utilize problem-solving skills, and further develop abilities in the field of Computer Science. Contact me with offers!
